Since 1966

SIX DECADES

Born in 1966 as Chevrolet's answer to the Ford Mustang, the Camaro didn't just enter the pony car wars — it rewrote the rules.

From the first-generation SS396 to the modern ZL1 1LE, every generation has pushed the boundary of what a street-legal performance car can do.

Today's ZL1 is the most powerful Camaro ever built — delivering 650 horsepower to the rear wheels.

Every surface serves a purpose. The wider body adds 80mm of track width. The functional hood extractor pulls heat from the supercharger. The front splitter generates real downforce. This isn't styling — it's aerodynamic engineering made visible.

Supercharged LT4 V8

The Heart
of the Beast

The LT4 is a 6.2-liter supercharged V8 that delivers 650 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque. An Eaton 1.7-liter supercharger force-feeds air into the direct-injected engine, producing a flat torque curve that pins you to the seat from idle to redline.

Paired with either a Tremec 6-speed manual or a 10-speed automatic with paddle shifters, the ZL1 delivers its power with surgical precision. Active rev matching, no-lift shift, and launch control come standard.
